Heart Disease Risk Prediction Using Machine Learning Techniques

About Us

Abstract

Heart disease is a leading global health concern, necessitating effective predictive systems for early detection. This project focuses on developing a machine learning-based model to assess heart disease risk by analyzing key medical attributes such as age, blood pressure, and cholesterol levels. Utilizing algorithms like Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and Decision Trees, the model achieved a prediction accuracy of 86.5%, outperforming traditional methods. By enabling early intervention and optimizing healthcare resources, this system enhances patient care and reduces costs. The project underscores the potential of machine learning in transforming healthcare, offering insights into heart disease risk factors for improved outcomes.

Mission

The mission of this project is to develop an accurate and efficient heart disease risk prediction model using machine learning algorithms. By analyzing medical attributes such as age, cholesterol levels, blood pressure, and chest pain type, the model identifies individuals at risk of heart disease, enabling early detection and intervention. Leveraging algorithms like Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and Decision Trees, the project aims to provide reliable predictions, support healthcare professionals in diagnosing patients, and optimize resource utilization. Ultimately, this initiative aspires to enhance patient outcomes, reduce healthcare costs, and contribute to the broader effort of combating heart disease globally.
